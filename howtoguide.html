Run or Die

Tactical Survival Guide: Era 66 Million BC

TYRANNOSAURUS REX

THREAT LEVEL: CRITICAL
  • Vision is NOT Based on Movement: Contrary to popular myth, the T-Rex has binocular vision better than a hawk. Freezing makes you a stationary target. Hide immediately.
  • Seek Dense Foliage: This apex predator is massive. It cannot navigate thick, tangled forests easily. If you hear the ground shaking, dive into the thickest brush you can find.
  • Do Not Outrun, Outmaneuver: You cannot outrun it (18-25 mph). However, its turn radius is terrible. Run in tight zig-zags around large trees or rocks to break its momentum.

VELOCIRAPTOR

THREAT LEVEL: HIGH (PACK HUNTER)
  • Protect Your Flanks: They are ambush predators. If you see one in front of you, the attack is coming from the sides. Put your back against a wall or cliff immediately.
  • Intimidate, Don't Run: Running triggers their chase instinct. Stand your ground, shout, throw rocks, and make yourself look large. They prefer easy prey.
  • Elevate: They are excellent jumpers, but they cannot climb smooth vertical surfaces well. Get to a high roof or a sheer rock face.

TRICERATOPS

THREAT LEVEL: EXTREME (TERRITORIAL)
  • Respect the Charge: This is not a gentle giant; it is a prehistoric rhino. If it lowers its head and paws the ground, you have seconds to move.
  • The Matador Defense: Do not run in a straight line away from it. Wait for the charge and dive perpendicular to its path at the last second. Its momentum prevents it from turning sharply.
  • Climb High: They are heavy and grounded. A tree or a large boulder is your best safety. They will not ram a large tree to get a small human.

MOSASAURUS

THREAT LEVEL: AQUATIC DEATH
  • Stay Out of Deep Water: If you cannot see the bottom, do not enter. This leviathan senses pressure changes in the water.
  • The Shoreline is Safe: Unlike crocodiles, Mosasaurs are strictly marine animals. They cannot come onto land. If you are pursued, swim frantically for the shallows and beach yourself.
  • Avoid Surface Splashing: Thrashing on the surface mimics a wounded animal. If you must swim, use smooth, quiet strokes.

PTERANODON

THREAT LEVEL: MEDIUM (AERIAL)
  • Watch the Skies: Death comes from above. If a shadow passes over you quickly, hit the dirt immediately.
  • Stay Under Canopy: They require open space to dive and swoop. Dense forests or canyons with overhangs are no-fly zones for them.
  • Fight Back: Their bones are hollow and fragile. If one lands to peck at you, a solid strike with a heavy branch can break a wing, grounding it permanently.
